- Status: Proposed

## Context

What forces are at play? What is the problem we are solving, and what
constraints shape the decision? Two or three paragraphs. No hedging.

## Decision

The decision, stated directly. One paragraph. A reader should be able to
answer "what did we decide" from this section alone.

## Consequences

What becomes easier. What becomes harder. What follow-on work this enables
or blocks. Be honest about tradeoffs.

## Alternatives considered

For each alternative, one paragraph: what it was, why we rejected it. This
section is the point of the ADR. It stops a future agent or contributor from
re-suggesting the rejected option.
